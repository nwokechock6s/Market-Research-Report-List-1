<p><p>An Optical Fiber Gyroscope (OFG) is a device that utilizes the interference of light to sense rotation. It operates on the principle of the Sagnac effect, where a beam of light traverses a coil of optical fiber in opposite directions. Any rotational movement causes a phase shift between the two light beams, allowing precise measurement of angular velocity. OFGs are highly regarded for their accuracy, reliability, and immunity to external factors such as temperature and magnetic fields, making them ideal for navigation in various applications, including aerospace, maritime, and defense.</p><p>The Optical Fiber Gyroscope Market is projected to experience robust expansion, with an expected growth rate of 11.3% CAGR during the forecast period. <p><strong>The Optical Fiber Gyroscope Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Interferometric</li><li>Resonant</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The optical fiber gyroscope market comprises three main types: interferometric, resonant, and other gyroscopes. Interferometric gyroscopes utilize light interference patterns to measure rotation, offering high precision and stability, making them ideal for aerospace and defense applications. Resonant gyroscopes, on the other hand, detect changes in resonance frequency due to rotation, providing compact and cost-effective solutions for consumer electronics and automotive systems. The "other" category includes hybrid and novel designs that combine features of both types to enhance performance across various industries.</p></p>
