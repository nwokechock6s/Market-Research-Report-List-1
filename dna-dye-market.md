<p><p>The DNA dye market comprises several key types including Ethidium Bromide, SYBR Stain, Propidium Iodide, and Hoechst Dye. Ethidium Bromide is widely used for visualizing nucleic acids in gels, while SYBR Stain allows for real-time PCR applications. Propidium Iodide is typically employed for cell cycle analysis, as it penetrates only dead cells, whereas Hoechst Dye selectively stains live cells and is useful for quantifying DNA content. Other dyes offer specific functionalities catering to niche applications in genomic research.</p></p>
