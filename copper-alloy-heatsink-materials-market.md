The main types include Cu-Mo (copper-molybdenum), ideal for high-performance electronics due to its low thermal expansion; Cu-Al (copper-aluminum), known for its lightweight and cost-effectiveness; Cu-Zr (copper-zirconium), which provides improved strength at elevated temperatures; Cu-Fe (copper-iron), valued for its magnetic properties; and Cu-W (copper-tungsten), used in high-temperature applications. Other alloys also cater to specialized thermal requirements, enhancing efficiency across various industries.</p></p>
