<p><p>The laboratory vacuum pumps market comprises various types, primarily focusing on dry vacuum pumps and rotary vane vacuum pumps. Dry vacuum pumps operate without the need for liquid lubrication, making them suitable for applications requiring clean environments. They provide consistent performance and low maintenance. In contrast, rotary vane vacuum pumps utilize rotating vanes to create a vacuum, offering higher pumping speeds and are often more cost-effective for laboratory settings. Both types play critical roles in various scientific and industrial applications.</p></p>
