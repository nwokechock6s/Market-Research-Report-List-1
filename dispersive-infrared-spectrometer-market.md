<p><p>The dispersive infrared spectrometer market is segmented into three types based on wavelength: Far Infrared Light, Mid-Infrared Light, and Near Infrared Light. Far infrared light (typically 30-1000 µm) is used for studying low-frequency molecular vibrations and solid-state physics. Mid-infrared light (2.5-30 µm) is vital for analyzing molecular structures and functional groups. Near infrared light (0.75-2.5 µm) interacts with overtone and combination vibrations, making it valuable for agricultural, pharmaceutical, and food quality analysis, among other applications.</p></p>
